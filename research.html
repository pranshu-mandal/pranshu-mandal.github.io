Current Research Focus

Priority: AI Applications in Signal Processing, Medical Implementation, and Computer Vision

My current research has evolved from astronomical instrumentation to focus on cutting-edge AI applications across multiple domains, leveraging my expertise in complex data analysis to solve real-world problems.

Predictive AI for Seizure Detection

Duration: 2023 - Present

Achievement: 92% sensitivity, 15-minute advance warning

Led development of a deep learning system for predictive seizure detection from EEG data. Created novel feature extraction techniques and real-time processing frameworks compatible with clinical equipment. The system achieved 92% sensitivity and 85% specificity in clinical validation, with potential for integration into wearable monitoring devices.

ChunkedPCA Algorithm Development

Duration: 2022 - 2025

Impact: 15% improvement in signal-to-noise ratio

Invented a novel machine learning-based approach for noise reduction in astronomical data. The algorithm uses segmented Principal Component Analysis optimized for time-series data, enabling detection of previously unobservable faint sources and removing detector cross-talk artifacts.

MKID Data Analysis Pipeline

Duration: 2018 - 2022

Impact: 30% sensitivity improvement, days to hours processing time

Developed comprehensive end-to-end processing workflows for the Nobeyama 45m Telescope MKID camera. Created automated calibration procedures, real-time monitoring systems, and modular architecture that became the standard processing method supporting multiple research teams.
